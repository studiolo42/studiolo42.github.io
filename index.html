Music
Music

Sound as the
Architecture
of Feeling

Music is the only art that exists entirely in time — you cannot revisit a moment the way you can look again at a painting. That makes it the most mysterious and the most human of all the arts.

BAROQUE · 1600–1750

Johann Sebastian Bach

The architecture of counterpoint — voices in mathematically perfect conversation. The Well-Tempered Clavier is the Bible of Western music: both a technical treatise and an act of profound beauty.

CLASSICAL · 1750–1820

Mozart & Haydn

The mastery of form — the sonata, the symphony, the string quartet. Mozart's late symphonies achieve a perfection of balance that has never been surpassed. Complexity made to feel effortless.

ROMANTIC · 1820–1900

Beethoven Onward

When Beethoven lost his hearing and kept composing, something new entered music: the idea that art could be a direct expression of the artist's inner struggle. The self became the subject.

20th CENTURY

Jazz & Ravel

Two parallel revolutions: Jazz brought rhythmic liberation and improvisational freedom from America's African heritage. Ravel brought harmonic colour of an almost painterly richness from France.

MINIMALISM

Arvo Pärt & Glass

After the maximalism of the 20th century, a return to stillness. Pärt's tintinnabuli method — music stripped to its barest harmonic bones — achieves a silence within sound.

"Without music, life would be a mistake."— Friedrich Nietzsche

Natural History

The World
As It Actually
Is

Natural history is the oldest human obsession — the desire to name, classify, and understand the living world. It requires the same careful attention as art, the same patience as music, and the same humility before something greater than yourself.

🦕

Deep Time

The Earth is 4.5 billion years old. Life has existed for 3.8 billion years. Homo sapiens have been here for 300,000 years — a single blink. Understanding geological time is perhaps the most radical shift in perspective available to any human mind. It doesn't diminish us; it contextualizes us.

The fossil record is the most extraordinary library ever discovered — a history of every creature that ever lived, written in stone. Every ammonite spiral is a chapter. Every trilobite is a sentence.

Tempus edax rerum — Time, the devourer of all things
🦋

Entomology

There are more species of beetle than of any other animal. The diversity of insect life is so vast as to be almost incomprehensible — and yet each species occupies a precise ecological niche.

Coleoptera · 400,000 species
🌿

Botany & Plant Intelligence

Plants communicate through underground fungal networks. Some trees live for thousands of years. The oldest known individual organism is a bristlecone pine — 5,000 years old.

Arbor vitae — Tree of life
🌊

The Ocean

95% of Earth's living space is ocean. We have better maps of the surface of Mars than of the ocean floor. The deep sea contains creatures that seem more alien than anything we have imagined.

Mare incognitum
🔬

Evolutionary Biology

Darwin's insight — descent with modification through natural selection — is the single most powerful idea in the history of biology. It connects every living thing to every other.

Omnis cellula e cellula
Light
Photography

Light Written
on Silver & Time

Photography means "writing with light" — from the Greek phōs (light) and graphé (writing). Every photograph is a sentence written by photons. The photographer chooses only when to let the light in, and for how long.

What interests me most is the paradox at photography's heart: it appears to capture objective reality — a moment as it actually was — but every photograph is a radical subjective act. What is included, what is excluded, where the light falls, what moment is chosen — each decision reflects a mind as much as a scene.

01

Light & Shadow

Chiaroscuro exists in photography as much as in Caravaggio. The relationship between what is illuminated and what is swallowed by darkness is the fundamental grammar of the image.

02

The Decisive Moment

Henri Cartier-Bresson called it l'instant décisif — the fraction of a second when form, content, and meaning align perfectly. Most photographers spend their lives hunting it.

03

Time Made Visible

Every photograph is also a document of entropy. The people in old photographs are all dead now. That knowledge transforms every image into something vertiginous — a moment stolen from time.

Books
Books & Literature

A Life
Spent in Other
Minds

Reading is the closest thing we have to time travel and telepathy combined. These are books that rewired how I see the world.

01 · NATURAL HISTORY

The Selfish Gene

RICHARD DAWKINS · 1976

Reframed evolution from the organism's perspective to the gene's. One of those books where you finish it and the world looks genuinely different.

02 · ART

The Shock of the New

ROBERT HUGHES · 1980

The definitive account of modern art — passionate, opinionated, brilliantly written. Hughes loved great art enough to defend it and hate bad art enough to say so.

03 · MUSIC

The Rest Is Noise

ALEX ROSS · 2007

The history of 20th-century music told as cultural and political history. Makes you hear Shostakovich differently when you understand what Stalin was doing to his country.

04 · SCIENCE

A Short History of Nearly Everything

BILL BRYSON · 2003

The best popular science book ever written — the history of how humans came to understand the universe, told with wonder, precision, and a great deal of affectionate comedy.

05 · PHILOSOPHY

The Birth of Tragedy

FRIEDRICH NIETZSCHE · 1872

Nietzsche's argument that the greatest art holds Dionysian chaos and Apollonian order in perfect tension. Written in a fever. Still undefeated as a theory of why art matters.

06 · BIOGRAPHY

Leonardo da Vinci

WALTER ISAACSON · 2017

The definitive modern biography of history's greatest polymath. What emerges is not a genius but a man of insatiable curiosity who refused to stop asking questions.
